1. NLPP with one equality constraint (two or three variables) using the method of Lagrange’s multipliers

\begin{equation} h = constraint \end{equation} \begin{equation} L(x_{1},x_{2},\lambda) = optimal - \lambda(constraint) \end{equation} \begin{equation} \Delta = \begin{vmatrix} 0 & {\frac{\mathrm \partial h}{\mathrm \partial x_{1}}}& {\frac{\mathrm \partial h}{\mathrm \partial x_{2}}} \\ {\frac{\mathrm \partial h}{\mathrm \partial x_{1}}} & {\frac{\mathrm \partial^2 L}{\mathrm \partial x_{1}^2}} & {\frac{\mathrm \partial^2 L}{\mathrm \partial x_{1} \partial x_{2}}} \\ {\frac{\mathrm \partial h}{\mathrm \partial x_{2}}} & {\frac{\mathrm \partial^2 L}{\mathrm \partial x_{1} \partial x_{2}}} & {\frac{\mathrm \partial^2 L}{\mathrm \partial x_{2}^2}} \end{vmatrix} \end{equation}
  • Δ -> positive then maxima
  • Δ -> negative then minima
