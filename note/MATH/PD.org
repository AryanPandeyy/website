* Poisson Distribution
** Formula
\begin{equation}
P(x) = e^{-m}\frac{m^x}{x!} .... x=0,1,2,..\infty
\end{equation}
** Mean and Variance
*** Mean
\begin{equation}
Mean = m
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\nonumber
m=nP
\end{equation}
*** Variance
\begin{equation}
\sigma^{2}=m
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\nonumber
\sigma= \sqrt{m}
\end{equation}
** Recurrence Relation
\begin{equation}
P(x+1)=P(x)\frac{m}{x+1}
\end{equation}
** Eqn
- Atleast one defective
  \begin{equation}
  P(X\geq4)=1-P(X<4)
  \end{equation}
  \begin{equation}
  \nonumber
  \therefore P(X\geq4)=1-[P(x=1)+P(x=2)+P(x=3)]
  \end{equation}
- Two n's are given, then take one as 'n' and other as 'N'
** Fitting of poisson distribution
\begin{equation}
mean=\frac{\Sigma{fx}}{\Sigma{f}} .. x=data, f=frquency
\end{equation}
